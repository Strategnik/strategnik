## DESIGN DIRECTION

**Brand palette:**
- Background: #111113 (near-black) or #0a0a0b (deep black)
- Primary accent: #1de2c4 (turquoise) — use for numbered badges, key stats, CTA sections
- Secondary accent: #0051ff (ink blue) — use sparingly for contrast highlights
- Text primary: #FFFFFF
- Text secondary: #9ca3af (gray-400)
- Card/section backgrounds: #1a1a1d or #18181b (subtle elevation over black)

**Typography:**
- Headlines: Söhne Breit Kräftig (bold), all caps or title case
- Body: Söhne Breit Buch (book weight), italic for emphasis
- Monospace accents (optional): Söhne Mono for stats or data callouts
- If Söhne unavailable in design tool: substitute Inter or Space Grotesk (headlines), DM Sans (body)

**Layout:**
- Portrait orientation (1080x1920 for social stories, or 1080x1350 for feed)
- Also produce a wider 1200x1800 or 16:9 variant for LinkedIn carousel / PDF
- Dark card-based grid layout — each of the 6 areas gets its own card
- Generous whitespace between sections
- No stock illustrations or cartoon avatars — use minimal geometric icons or simple line icons

**Visual style:**
- Clean, dark, editorial — think pitch deck, not blog infographic
- No gradients except subtle turquoise glow behind key stats
- Thin borders (#2a2a2d) on cards if needed for separation
- Turquoise numbered badges (filled circles) for each step

## THE 6 LAYERS — "THE INTELLIGENCE LAYER"

> **Layout:** Section header: "WHAT AGENTS ACTUALLY NEED TO OPERATE" in turquoise, caps. Then a 3x2 grid of cards. Each card is a dark elevated rectangle (#1a1a1d) with a turquoise numbered badge.

> **Section intro text (gray-300):**
Before an agent can run your marketing, it needs to understand your brand the way your best marketer does. That means building six layers of context most companies don't have — and can't buy off the shelf.

> **Icons:** One simple line icon per card. Phosphor Icons, Lucide, or Tabler — thin/light weight, white or turquoise stroke.

---

### Card 1

> **Icon:** Fingerprint or voice waveform (brand identity)

**Number badge:** 1
**Title:** BRAND CONTEXT

- Voice, tone, editorial standards — encoded so agents produce on-brand work without prompting every time
- Not a style guide PDF. A machine-readable operating spec that agents reference autonomously.
- Your brand becomes infrastructure, not a document someone forgets to check.

> **Bold/turquoise highlight:** "machine-readable operating spec"

---

### Card 2

> **Icon:** Crosshair / target with user silhouette (ICP)

**Number badge:** 2
**Title:** MARKET CONTEXT

- ICP definitions, buying committee maps, competitive positioning, account prioritization
- Agents need to know who to talk to, why they care, and what the alternatives look like
- This is the strategic intelligence that turns generic outreach into precision targeting

> **Bold/turquoise highlight:** "precision targeting"

---

### Card 3

> **Icon:** Database with connected nodes (data architecture)

**Number badge:** 3
**Title:** DATA ARCHITECTURE

- CRM structure, product signal mapping, lead scoring, behavioral triggers
- Agents don't run campaigns — they respond to signals. Your data has to emit the right ones.
- Bad data doesn't just slow you down. It trains agents to do the wrong things faster.

> **Bold/turquoise highlight:** "respond to signals"

---

### Card 4

> **Icon:** Factory/assembly line or template grid (content systems)

**Number badge:** 4
**Title:** CONTENT INFRASTRUCTURE

- Not content. The system that produces content — templates, schemas, production frameworks, editorial workflows
- Built so agents can generate, adapt, and deploy content across channels without starting from scratch every time
- The difference between "write me a blog post" and a machine that publishes with intent

> **Bold/turquoise highlight:** "publishes with intent"

---

### Card 5

> **Icon:** Network/hub-and-spoke diagram (distribution)

**Number badge:** 5
**Title:** DISTRIBUTION ORCHESTRATION

- Multi-platform signal routing — CRM, landing pages, email, ads, workflows — all connected
- Agents deploy messaging across the digital ecosystem in vectors: clusters of relevant activity where your brand shows up with style and timing
- Not channel management. Ecosystem presence.

> **Bold/turquoise highlight:** "clusters of relevant activity"

---

### Card 6

> **Icon:** Gauge/dashboard or telescope (measurement + steering)

**Number badge:** 6
**Title:** MEASUREMENT & STEERING

- Revenue influence, journey conversion, time-to-personalization, decision accuracy
- Not dashboards that report what happened. Feedback loops that steer what happens next.
- The agent fleet learns. The system adapts. Marketing becomes a precision instrument.

> **Bold/turquoise highlight:** "feedback loops that steer what happens next"

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Output formats needed
- **Social (LinkedIn/X feed):** 1080 x 1350px (4:5)
- **Social (stories/vertical):** 1080 x 1920px (9:16) — may need to split into carousel
- **PDF one-pager:** Letter size (8.5 x 11) or A4 — for email / sales follow-up
- **LinkedIn carousel:** Split into 8–10 slides at 1080 x 1080px or 1080 x 1350px

### Carousel slide breakdown (if splitting)
1. Header — "Your AI Is Still a Chatbot"
2. Provocation block — "That's not transformation. That's a typing upgrade."
3. The Question — "How much is your AI doing for you?"
4. Layers 1–2 — Brand Context + Market Context
5. Layers 3–4 — Data Architecture + Content Infrastructure
6. Layers 5–6 — Distribution Orchestration + Measurement & Steering
7. The Contrast table — What you have vs. what you need
8. Why You Can't Hire This — the 4 disciplines
9. CTA — The Intelligence Layer Diagnostic

### What NOT to do
- No stock photos of people
- No AI-generated character illustrations (no cartoon CMOs, no robot mascots)
- No gradients or glassmorphism
- No light/white backgrounds — this is a dark-brand asset
- Don't soften the language — the directness is the brand
- Don't use the word "agentic" in headlines — it's overused and the infographic itself argues against buzzword labeling
- No "AI strategy" framing — this is infrastructure, not advice
